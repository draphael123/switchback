A NARROW-GAUGE LINE UP A MOUNTAIN
A railway can only climb so hard. Yours manages 6 in 100 — and the mountain between Lowthwaite and Crag End is nearly three times that.

So you cannot go straight at it. You go the long way round: out across the hillside, round the head of the valley, and back again — cutting and filling to hold your grade the whole way.

Then you set the engine going and watch it work.
